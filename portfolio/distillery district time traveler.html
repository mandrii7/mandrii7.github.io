Distillery District Time Traveler

"Distillery District Time Traveler" is the project that I and two other group members created during the Fall 2023 semester for the Interactive Systems Project 1 course in the Interaction Design program at George Brown College.

This project is an idea and a prototype of Distillery District's official application with a unique AR feature called Time Traveler. Using the navigation tab at the bottom of each page user can acces five main pages, which are Home, Search, Gallery, Profile, and the most important page of this application Time Traveler. Navigate to the "Home" page to discover a detailed list of upcoming events in the district, complete with relevant photos and dates. Utilize the "Search" page, equipped with a search engine, to explore district restaurants, shops, and attractions, accessing comprehensive information about specific locations. Explore the rich array of photos showcasing the Distillery District on the "Gallery" page. Manage all editable user information, such as details, notifications, saved favorite places, events, and settings, on the "Profile" page.

Our application's standout feature is the "Time Traveler," setting it apart as a distinctive offering. This augmented reality (AR) capability allows users to point their device's camera at any structure in the Distillery District. After scanning the chosen building, the application provides two interaction choices: "History" and "Time Traveler." Opting for the "History" button seamlessly transports users to the official Distillery District website, offering in-depth information about the historical background of the scanned building. Meanwhile, selecting the "Time Traveler" button triggers the application to generate a three-dimensional (3D) model depicting the appearance of the scanned building from a medieval era. Users can zoom in, zoom out, rotate, or remove the model entirely from their screen.

The "Distillery District Time Traveler" received positive feedback during its presentation from Interactive Systems Project 1 professors Christopher Pandolfi & Eirene Keh for the uniqueness of the idea, high posibility of potential creation, clean design, and potential benefits for the district.
