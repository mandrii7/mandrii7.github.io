Wall of Fortune
January 2023 to May 2023

An interactive art installation that brings a sense of community

and childhood flashbacks through a simplicity of randomly

exchanging notes with each other.

Overview
Role & Responsibilities

I actively participated in brainstorming and researching features for

our project, contributed to creating the mood board, developed

Arduino code, and helped in the physical assembly alongside

my team. I also presented our project at the George Brown's

"YES!" exhibition at the end of the winter 2023 semester.

Project Type
Interactive Art Installation
Digital Tools
Figma, Illustrator, Arduino
Project Description

This project is an interactive art piece consisting of a wall that has lots of small doors, each numbered from one to

twenty-four. Behind every door, there's a note with a wish or call to action. Visitors start the activity by pressing

a button next to the wall. Once the button is pressed, the wall will choose one of the doors to light up

randomly. Visitor then takes the note from the chosen door, keeps it, and writes a new note on a

piece of paper for the next person to find.

Starting Point
Requirements

1) It should be possible to set up this project within the walls of the college.

2) The whole construction of this project should not exceed the budget

of 400$ provided by the college, and all materials should be possible

to find and buy;

3) The project should include some kind of skills that were learned

throughout the Physical Interfaces course;

4) It can be created with both physical and digital tools;

5) The project should be interactive;

Goals

1) An interactive structure that brings back old memories;

2) Makes people remember that we are not alone and that

other people might be experiencing same things at that

moment;

3) Let people participate in the activity together.

4) Use materials that can be easily recycled;

5) Make a memorable experience;

6) Make it fun for everyone;

Idea Development
Research

The first step of our project was the assignment called

"Precedent Study", which required each group to research 10

different exhibitions, installations, technologies, and study

them. We were supposed to research the year it was created,

the intention for the project, how it works, the main purpose of

it, what the user experiences, and how it makes people feel.

The main purposes of this assignment were to inspire us, make us

understand the important components of such a project, and to

research the potential features of our own idea. The image

on the right is a visual compilation of the exhibitions,

installations, and technologies that our group has studied.

First Idea

After we conducted research for our first assignment

and gained some inspiration, our team came up with the

initial raw idea for our project. It is essentially the

same concept that we ultimately chose and executed, but

we were considering allowing two people to interact

simultaneously: one person by pressing a button and the

second by jumping on a platform. The image on the left

is as simple sketch of this initial idea.

Moodboard

Our next step was creating a moodboard that would display five

aspects of our project, which are form, lighting, tech/electronics,

purpose, and materials. In the form section, you can see simple

numbered boxes from which we gained our inspiration. The

lighting section shows simple interactive lighting from different

exhibitions. In the tech/electronics section, we have added

buttons and sensors. The purpose section has pictures of union,

interaction, collaboration, and pictures that display pollution.

And the last section, which is materials, shows pictures

of wood, cardboard, and straws as materials that we chose

for the construction of our installation.

Storyboard

At this point, we already needed to figure out what the whole

experience is going to look like for the user. After conducting

a couple more group meetings and agreeing on the final list of

features, interactions, and how the user journey is going to

look, we created a simple storyboard drawing of the interaction

process. This step helped to visualize our ideas, making them

clearer for us and for the professors, and provided a final

look of what exactly we are going to build.

Conceptual Sketches

The last major aspect of the idea development process was

figuring out the dimensions of our installation, the sizes

of each box separately, box handle sizes, the size of the

holes into which the lighting is directed, and the final

look of the installation set up at the exhibition. The

image on the right is a simple sketch of everything

I mentioned above.

Final Touches

After finishing essentially the entire idea development process, there was just a minor task left:

designing the appearance of our notes and the logo for our project.

One of these logos was eventually printed on the top of the installation and on the description/instructions board.

Construction
Materials List

Before starting the actual physical creation of our

project, every team was supposed to present a materials

list to professors so they could approve it and provide

all the needed components. Project construction shouldn't

have exceeded the budget of $400 (not including the materials

that could have been borrowed from the college). Along with

the materials list, every group was supposed to provide a

list of links to the sources where it is possible to buy

every single construction component.

Physical Creation

After the professors had approved our materials list and provided every single component

that we asked for, our team started the physical creation of the installation.

The whole construction process took us 5 days to finish, and our project

was ready to be showcased at the exhibition.

Summary
Results

"Wall of Fortune" was showcased at "YES!", the official

show for George Brown College's School of Design. It

received positive feedback for its ease of

understanding, interactivity, unique

concept, and recyclable design.

Insights

Through this project, I've discovered that a team of five can create something

that is both thoughtful and professional in a relatively short timeframe. The

key insight from this project and exhibition is that the number of features

and the complexity of a project aren't always the most important factors.

Our project received more favorable feedback from visitors at the exhibition

compared to other, more complex entries, largely due to its simplicity and

ease of understanding.

Potential Improvements

We would have improved the materials and quality of the entire prototype, making it more durable because users

broke the button a few times during the exhibition. We would probably also add some sort of sound effects

when the user pressed a button, when the wall was choosing one of the doors, and when the door was

finally chosen. Additionally, we would have improved the Arduino code due to its imperfections,

stemming from our beginner-level knowledge in this area at the time.
